How mathematics determined my reading process

How do I increase my chance to read just page turners?

Actually the mathematical basics are pretty simple: The more books you try the better the chance to find a addictive one.

How many pages should I read in one go?

I decided for 25 because 100 can be easily divided by four and its a quantity that my brain registers as easy to read.

How to recognize the books I am just reading?

I use here a line or stripe system that resembles binary numbers. So I can identify by the stripes on the pages I read which book it is. Very important when I found a pageturner, that I might want to read in one go and also when I found a book I dont like so I can junk it quickly and get place for more.
