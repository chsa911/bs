Donnells Pocahontas brings me to Frankfurt today and I love it. Great read. The last years of the indians

Krakauers "into thin air" all day long

Krakauers "into thin air" fills my day and I am delighted that the book doen t disappoint. The ultimate page turner

I prepare new books for reading and I start the one that I am looking forward the most. The ultimate book. I started. Krakauers "into thin air" today and am want to read the whole book asap. Another book that I discoverd was Kennedy's "the domino complot"

23.:

Travelling home means a lot of reading and its a book that I found in frankfurt in the street(somebody put them in street to give away) that delights me the most. Sherry Argovs "Why the nicest men remain with the worst women" that delights me the most. A great read. But I also finished Moravias "master stories", Bertholds "the long treck" Links lonely night, slaughters "emergency room", Illies "1913", Peters "the devils novice", hosseinis "one thousand splendid suns". Waltaris Minutus also found its end. Great trip great reading. Perfect.

22.:

Left Minutus at home so I discovered Moravia's "masterstories" and Charlotte Link's "Lonely night". A pageturner that gets me super thrilled. During the day I also discovered slaughter's "emergency room"

21.:

Commute reading. Waltari's "Minutus" brings me through the day.

20.:

Office day means train and tram read. I finished Sigmund Freud and discovered Waltari's "Minutus the Roman". What a great reading day.

19.:

Georg Markus's title "Sigmund Freud and the secret of the soul" is the next highlight I discovered today and which brought me through the day. Great read.

18.:

Day to relax and preparing for the trip to frankfurt. Travelling in the train I could finish M.Z. Thomas "Marco Polo and the sons of the sky". During the trip I could read: Jose Carreras "Singing with the soule". Gaedes "hellsride to bujumbura". buchheims "luxuryliner", todenhöfers: "why are you killing zaid?". blickensdörfers the "sons of the war", daniel steel "echoes" and adventure from the publisher arena. So it was a trip full of great adventure. A trip on a luxury cruiser, 1. world war story, adventures in the antarktica and in asia. What more could you want. Almost like Marco Polo travelling through time and space.

17.:

Saturday morning. I slept in today and took a trip to munich I could read cooks book "coma" and prestons thriller "codex". These do didn t disappoint and were real pageturners. A great day from munich to the Starnberger Lake reading and getting fresh air.

16.:

Trip back home. Today I went from Frankfurt to Munich so I could read a lot. I read downers: "last concubine", cerams: "the american", konsaliks "dschunkendoctor", robbins "the wilds" and xialongs "blood and red silk". A great reading trip and I almost finished all books I had brought from munich on last sunday.

15.:

I finished wickerts book on the way home and organised my books for the trip tomorrow. I hope to read a lot on my ride to munich.

14.:

After reading Richistan I discovered a new highlight with wickerts "the honest is the stupid". It explains exactly the state of germany today and shows the reasons why it came to this crazy situation. Definitly recommendable.

13.:

The "last concubine" from lesley downer gave me a great ride to our office in frankfurt in the morning. During the day I discovered "Richistan" from Robert Frank a pageturner that I read all evening long and couldn t lay down.

A great book about the life of the rich and ultra rich. The great feeling reading this title justified all the work I put into my book procurement and reading technique.

12.:

Last night I just "ate" gerald greens "holocaust" which just got me hooked. Even though it is pretty depressing it is a great read and saved my day.
